Global Sisters Program

This project aims to build capabilities and economic resilience among ASEAN women entrepreneurs particularly in the Philippines and Indonesia. This project is also an opportunity to connect ASEAN women entrepreneurs to Australia. The project commence in June 2022 and will run until May 2023 and all participation will be virtual.

Papuan sisters led by Lucia Runggeari, who has been assisted by Global sisters, have conducted peer-to-peer education sessions. The aim of these sessions, as stated by Global sisters: aim to make business possible and create long-term economic security for women in Indonesia and the Philippines.

The participants of Better Together Papuan Sister are five business women in Papua who have different backgrounds in running their business. They are Elke who leads BUMKAM Hentachi Holtekamp and she is working to empower local people in Holtekamp Jayapura.Currently, Nani is a director of Noken Foundation who has passion in developing her pig farm. Moreover, in this program we have Elsa, she is a staff in the Noken Foundation who runs a business for women's accessories. Naomi is a journalist of one International News and who has a hobby of creating women's accessories such as earrings and necklaces .From her hobby she has tried to sell to some friends and now becomes her business called Lerovi’os collection. The last participant is Grace Sumihe, owner of Gracycurls. As a teacher of Sekolah Papua Kasih, she is enthusiastic to teach Papuan students about business. Her first problem with hair brought the New idea of developing natural products.
